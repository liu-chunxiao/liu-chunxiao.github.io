Random crystal corner

A crystal of size L×L×L is fully filled with atoms. From one corner, atoms are removed under the constraint that if a cube is missing then all cubes deeper into the corner must also be missing. The missing region therefore forms a 3-dimensional Young diagram (a plane partition). Monte-Carlo sampling generates typical thermal crystal corners.


连连看 (Lianliankan)

Pick two identical tiles.
Rule: two identical tiles vanish if they can be connected through empty space by an axis-aligned path with ≤ 2 turns.
New behavior: PBC (torus) + one empty padding row/column is always present.

Radial KPZ / Eden Growth

Start from either a random closed curve or a regular polygon, then let the interface grow outward according to different stochastic rules. The isotropic rule tends toward a circular macroscopic shape, while anisotropic rules can produce kinetic Wulff-like shapes. The “corner growth” option is a simple LPP-inspired toy rule rather than the exact integrable model.
